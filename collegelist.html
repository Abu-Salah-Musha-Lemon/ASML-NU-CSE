Distinctive Features of the National University's Computer Science and Engineering
Program : A Closer Look at Semesters, Examinations, and Project Work
  1. The National University has a total of 8 semesters in its Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) program. Although each semester lasts for 6 months, it never concludes precisely in 6 months. The duration sometimes varies, but most of the time, it extends from the 7th to the 8th month.
  2. The examination method at the National University differs from that of public or private universities. In this context, the semester final exam is conducted for 80 marks, with 20 marks allocated for college in-course assessments and attendance.
  3. The examination system at the National University is not similar to that of public or private universities. Here, the semester final exam is directly held without any mid-term exams. The public and private universities often have mid-term exams, but the National University does not follow this practice. (But some institutes conduct midterm or in-course exams for better results of students.)
  4. In the 8th semester, there is a project/thesis in the syllabus, but the choice of project topics is flexible. Occasionally, there is also a thesis.
